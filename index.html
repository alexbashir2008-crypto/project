Detailed Report: Escalation of the Iran-Israel-US War

1. The Core Conflict and Direct Confrontation

The prolonged shadow war between Iran and Israel has escalated into a direct military conflict, drawing in United States forces. Following the breakdown of previous ceasefire agreements, US and Israeli forces initiated a massive joint campaign, codenamed Operation Epic Fury, directly targeting the Iranian leadership and core strategic assets.

2. Strategic Air Campaign Against Iranian Infrastructure

Under direct directives from Washington, the US Central Command (CENTCOM) and the Israeli Air Force launched intensive waves of airstrikes. Over 140 military installations across southern Iran—including major coastal defense nodes at Bandar Abbas, Qeshm Island, Jask, and Sirik—were heavily bombarded. The strikes successfully neutralized key Iranian drone manufacturing plants, long-range missile launch facilities, and coastal air-defense radar systems.

3. Closure of the Strait of Hormuz and Naval Blockade

In defiance of the air campaign, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) declared the complete closure of the Strait of Hormuz. Iranian naval forces deployed anti-ship cruise missiles against commercial cargo vessels attempting to transit the strategic waterway without authorization. This total blockade halts nearly 20% of the world's daily petroleum shipments, triggering severe international concern over energy security.

4. Iranian Retaliation Across Gulf Nations

Responding to the devastating airstrikes, Tehran unleashed a large-scale barrage of ballistic missiles and explosive suicide drones aimed at US military assets across the Middle East. Red alerts and air raid sirens were activated in Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, and Jordan. While Jordanian air defense systems intercepted multiple projectiles, several impacts were reported near allied installations, forcing regional forces into high-alert combat postures.

5. Global Economic Fallouts and Supply Chain Disruption

The sudden shutdown of vital maritime trade corridors sent shockwaves through international financial markets. Brent Crude oil prices surged instantly by over 4%, climbing steadily toward $79 per barrel. Global shipping conglomerates and international airlines have begun rerouting assets away from Middle Eastern airspace and waters, indicating massive upcoming delays and inflationary pressure on global supply chains.
